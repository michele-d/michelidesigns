A little bit about me

My journey into tech started with a degree in Communication Sciences and Disorders and a fellowship in Assistive Technology and Alternative Communication (think Stephen Hawking, though, unfortunately, I never had the honor of working with him). I worked for many years in a variety of roles as a speech pathologist, but was never happier than when I was creating new communication symbols or designing visual displays for augmentative communication systems.

Learning about the growing demand for professionals in the field UX/UI design peaked my passion and curiosity, and inspired me to begin classes in website development and design. In building my first website, I rediscovered the artistry and creativity I was missing in my work. Now I've made the switch to Web Design and Development where I can use the empathy, analytical and problem-solving skills refined as an educator, to create engaging and beautiful platforms that put all users first.

Hand-Coded HTML

My focus is writing clean, well-formatted, semantic HTML5 by hand to make sure that the content is easy to read, easy to collaborate, trouble-shoot and accessible.

Well-Organized CSS

I pride myself on writing CSS that is easy to read and build on. I focus on keeping my CSS lean and fast to load, and I make it a habit to stay up to date on current best practices.

Ease Converting Designs into Code

You can trust me to take a designer's PSD and quickly & accurately convert it into a webpage that is pixel-perfect match, that users will navigate with ease.
